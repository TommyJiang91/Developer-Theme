1. Introduction

The risk of online recruitment fraud arises as companies are highly dependent on online and automated systems in the hiring processes. Such fraud not only negatively impact job seekers, but also causes harm to the reputation of the hiring organizations.

Our motivation is to build an effective classification model to identify fake job postings to prevent any potential risk of the frauds. For this project, we used advanced word embedding techniques and neural network models and bring in additional predictors to identify fake job postings more effectively.

2. Data

Our project uses the “Real or Fake: Fake Job Description Prediction” dataset from Kaggle, which contains 18 thousand job descriptions across 18 features with disk size of 50.1 MB. The data is imbalanced with only 866 fake postings. Below is the word cloud of the fake job postings from the dataset.

3. Project Highlights

  • Improved the F1 score for both logistic regression and random forecast model by adding the extra Salary feature engineered using dataset from United States National Occupational Employment and Wage Estimates.

  • Experimented different embedding methods on text columns, among which the Count Vectorizer has the best performance for simpler models (both logistic regression and random forecast model).

  • Implemented neural network model with Count Vectorizer embedding and LSTM (long short term memory network) with Keras embedding for the classification task. The model structure and test result for both methods are shown below.

    • Neural Network Structures:

    Neural Network with LSTM Structure

  • Implemented Neutral network model with Elmo embedding.

4. Findings

  • In our dataset, the Count Vectorizer outperformed Tokenizer, which indicates that fake job postings use similar words, but the wording and paraphrasing of the content has little explanation power of whether the job posting is fake or real.

  • Neural network model has better performance than regular models such as random forest model with F1 score of 0.79 vs 0.65.

5. Future Projects

  • The underperformance of Elmo embedding might be due to the noise in the content of our job posting dataset. I would like to explore Elmo further in another text-only dataset.
